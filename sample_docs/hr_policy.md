# Northstar Support Systems - Employee Handbook Extract

> This policy belongs to a fictional company created for demonstration purposes.

## Remote work

Employees based in Sweden may work remotely for up to three days per week with manager approval. Teams must maintain enough office coverage for customer meetings, onboarding, and operational work.

Core collaboration hours are 09:30 to 15:00 Central European Time on normal working days. Employees may arrange the rest of their schedule with their manager. Each team holds one shared office day per month, announced at least two weeks in advance.

Remote work from another country requires written approval from People Operations before travel because tax, insurance, and employment rules may apply.

## Annual and sick leave

Full-time employees receive 25 paid vacation days per calendar year. Requests for more than three consecutive days should normally be submitted at least two weeks in advance. Approval depends on team coverage and previously approved leave.

Employees who are sick should notify their manager by 09:00 and record the absence in the HR system. Medical details should not be posted in team chat.

## Equipment policy

Northstar provides a company laptop, one monitor, a keyboard, a mouse, and a headset for each full-time employee. Employees may claim up to SEK 3,000 every three years for approved ergonomic accessories. Receipts and manager approval are required for reimbursement.

Company equipment remains Northstar property. Loss, theft, or damage must be reported to IT as soon as possible. Devices must use full-disk encryption, automatic screen locking, and the company endpoint-management software.

## Onboarding

New employees complete security awareness, privacy, and support-process training during their first week. Managers assign an onboarding partner and prepare a 30-day plan. Access is granted according to the employee's role and must not be shared with another person.

During offboarding, employees return company equipment and lose system access no later than the end of their final working day.
